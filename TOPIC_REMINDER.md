# Nudge - Topic & Subject Matter Reminder

## Overview

Nudge is a spaced repetition study reminder that helps you maintain and improve your knowledge on any topic through scientifically-proven review intervals. Instead of forgetting what you've learned, Nudge reminds you exactly when to review it for optimal retention and mastery.

## The Problem with Traditional Learning

When you study a topic:
- You feel confident immediately after learning it
- Days later, you've already forgotten key concepts
- Weeks later, recalling information feels like starting from scratch

**Result:** You study more topics, but retain less knowledge.

## The Solution: Spaced Repetition

Nudge uses the **forgetting curve** principle - you review topics at scientifically-optimized intervals:

- **Day 1**: Initial study/learning
- **Day 3**: Review the concepts (2 days later)
- **Day 7**: Review again (4 days later)
- **Day 14**: Another review (7 days later)
- **Day 30**: Deep review (16 days later)
- **Day 60**: Challenge yourself again (30 days later)
- **Day 120**: Final reinforcement (60 days later)

After 120 days, the topic is marked as **"mastered"** and continues at 120-day intervals for long-term retention.

## How to Use Nudge for Any Topic

### 1. Add a Topic After Learning It

When you learn something new:

1. Open Nudge
2. Click **"Add Item"**
3. Enter the topic details:
   - **Name:** e.g., "Python Decorators", "Database Indexing", "French Subjunctive"
   - **Tags:** Use tags like:
     - `Beginner` or `Advanced` (difficulty)
     - `Python`, `Database`, `French` (subject)
     - `Week-1` (study group)

Example:
```
Item Name: Machine Learning - Neural Networks
Tags: Advanced, AI, Week-2
```

### 2. Daily Review Routine

Every morning, open Nudge and check what's due:

- **Red highlighted items:** Topics overdue for review
- **Yellow highlighted items:** Topics due today
- Review each topic and quiz yourself on key concepts
- Click **"Mark as Reviewed"** to schedule the next review

### 3. Review Tips

When reviewing a topic:

- **First review (Day 3):** Summarize key concepts from memory, review notes if needed
- **Second review (Day 7):** Teach the concept to someone else or write an explanation
- **Third review (Day 14):** Apply the knowledge in a practical scenario
- **Later reviews:** Challenge yourself with complex problems or variations
